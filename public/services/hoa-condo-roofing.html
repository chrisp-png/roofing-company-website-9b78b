Why Association Roofing Is Different

Roofing projects for HOAs and condo associations are fundamentally different from single-family residential or straightforward commercial work. The complexity extends far beyond the technical installation:

Multiple Decision-Makers & Approval Processes

Association projects typically require board approval, membership votes, and sometimes lender or government agency sign-off. Proposals must be clear, detailed, and structured for non-technical stakeholders to understand and compare.

Budget Constraints & Reserve Funding

Many associations operate on tight budgets with limited reserves. Projects must be scoped to balance code requirements, longevity, and affordability — often requiring phasing, financing arrangements, or creative solutions to make the project financially feasible.

Resident Communication & Expectations

Association members are directly impacted by noise, parking restrictions, safety measures, and project timelines. Clear communication, realistic scheduling, and professional conduct are essential to minimize complaints and maintain positive relationships.

Occupied Building Logistics

Unlike vacant properties, association roofing work happens while people live, work, and move through the buildings. This requires careful staging, traffic control, safety protocols, and coordination with building operations to avoid disrupting daily life.

Detailed Documentation & Transparency

Boards and property managers need comprehensive documentation including engineering reports, progress photos, budget tracking, and inspection records. This transparency builds trust and helps associations meet their fiduciary responsibilities to membership.

All Phase Construction USA has extensive experience navigating these complexities. We've completed dozens of association roofing projects ranging from small townhome communities to large condo towers, and we understand what boards and property managers need to make informed decisions and execute successful projects.

Our Process for HOAs & Condos

All Phase Construction USA follows a structured, transparent process designed specifically for association roofing projects:

1. Initial Inspection & Assessment Report

We conduct a thorough roof inspection documenting existing conditions, code compliance issues, damage, and remaining service life. Our assessment includes:

  • • Comprehensive photo documentation of all roof areas and problem locations
  • • Written report detailing findings, recommendations, and prioritization of needed work
  • • Estimated remaining service life and repair vs. replacement analysis
  • • Code compliance review and identification of required upgrades

2. Detailed Scope of Work & Pricing Tiers

We develop line-item proposals with multiple options so boards can evaluate trade-offs between cost, performance, and longevity. Our proposals include:

  • • Good / Better / Best / Premium pricing tiers (similar to our Roof Cost Calculator)
  • • Breakdown of materials, labor, permits, engineering, and contingencies
  • • Warranty comparisons for different material and installation options
  • • Phasing options if the project needs to be spread across multiple budget cycles

3. Board Presentations & Member Communication

We attend board meetings to present findings, answer questions, and explain technical details in plain language. For larger projects requiring membership approval, we can provide presentation materials, FAQs, and cost-benefit summaries for distribution to unit owners.

4. Engineering & Permitting

As a dual-licensed contractor, we handle all engineering drawings, structural calculations, and permit applications required for HVHZ-compliant roofing work. This includes wind-load analysis, uplift calculations, and structural certifications when needed for financing or regulatory approval.

5. Pre-Construction Planning & Resident Notification

Before work begins, we develop a comprehensive plan addressing:

  • • Project timeline and phasing schedule
  • • Staging areas for materials and equipment
  • • Parking restrictions and traffic flow management
  • • Safety protocols and restricted access areas
  • • Communication plan including resident notices, hotline numbers, and daily updates

6. Phased Installation & Progress Reporting

Large association projects are often completed in phases by building, wing, or section. We provide regular progress updates including photo documentation, budget tracking, and schedule adherence to keep boards and property managers informed throughout the project.

7. Final Inspections & Close-Out Package

Upon completion, we deliver a comprehensive close-out package including:

  • • Final building department inspection reports and certificates of occupancy
  • • Manufacturer product warranties and installation certifications
  • • Wind-mitigation documentation for insurance premium reduction
  • • As-built drawings and maintenance guidelines
  • • Final photo documentation showing completed work

Coordination With Financing & State Programs

Many HOAs and condo associations require external financing or seek assistance through state programs to fund major roofing projects. All Phase Construction USA works alongside financing partners and program administrators to ensure your project meets eligibility requirements and documentation standards.

Special Assessment Financing

For associations without adequate reserves, special assessment financing allows the cost of roof replacement to be spread over multiple years through member assessments or building loans. We provide the detailed scope, engineering, and cost documentation lenders require to approve financing.

My Safe Florida Condo Program

The State of Florida offers programs like My Safe Florida Condo to help eligible condominium associations fund wind-mitigation and structural improvements, including roof replacement. Eligibility, funding levels, and application processes are controlled by the State and can change based on legislative action and available funding.

All Phase Construction USA can coordinate with program administrators and provide the technical documentation required for applications. However, we always recommend that associations verify current program status, eligibility requirements, and funding availability by visiting the official state websites or contacting program representatives directly.

Important: Program eligibility and grant amounts are determined by the State of Florida and are subject to change. We encourage boards to research these programs early in the planning process to understand what assistance may be available.

My Safe Florida Home Program

Similar to the condo program, My Safe Florida Home provides matching grants and subsidized inspections for eligible homeowners seeking wind-mitigation upgrades including roof replacement. This program is typically available to single-family homeowners and some townhome associations.

As with all state programs, eligibility criteria, grant amounts, and availability vary and are managed by the Florida Department of Financial Services. We recommend checking the official program website for current information before planning projects that depend on these funds.

Working With Your Financing Partner

If your association has already secured financing or is working with a lender, we coordinate directly with financing companies to provide draw schedules, progress inspections, lien releases, and final documentation required for fund disbursement.

Roof Systems for Associations

All Phase Construction USA installs all major roofing systems suitable for HOA and condo association properties:

Architectural Shingle Systems

Cost-effective and versatile, shingle roofs are popular for townhome communities and small multi-family properties. We install HVHZ-compliant systems with wind warranties up to 160 mph where available. Learn more about shingle roofing.

Concrete & Clay Tile Systems

Tile roofs offer exceptional durability and curb appeal for associations seeking 30–50+ year lifespan and minimal maintenance. Premium clay and composite options are available for high-end communities. Learn more about tile roofing.

Metal Roofing (Standing Seam & Mechanically Seamed)

Metal roofs provide superior wind resistance, energy efficiency, and longevity (40–70 years). Ideal for coastal associations and communities prioritizing low lifecycle costs. Learn more about metal roofing.

Flat & Low-Slope Systems (TPO, PVC, Modified Bitumen)

For condo buildings and commercial-style associations, we install high-performance membrane systems engineered for South Florida's demanding climate. Tapered insulation systems eliminate ponding and extend roof life. Learn more about flat roofing.

Deliverables for Boards & Lenders

All Phase Construction USA provides the comprehensive documentation that boards, property managers, and lenders require for association roofing projects:

  • • Engineering Reports & Structural Certifications: Required for financing, permitting, and some state programs. As a dual-licensed contractor, we handle structural engineering for wind-mitigation upgrades that most roofing-only contractors cannot legally perform.
  • • Building Permits & Inspection Records: Complete permit documentation and signed-off inspection reports proving code compliance.
  • • Progress Photography & Reporting: Regular photo updates documenting tear-off, substrate condition, waterproofing details, and final installation.
  • • Wind Mitigation Forms (OIR-B1-1802): Official documentation that qualifies the association or individual unit owners for insurance premium reductions of 20–45%.

  • • As-Built Drawings & Maintenance Guidelines: Documentation of the completed roof system for future reference and maintenance planning.
  • • Lien Releases & Final Affidavits: Legal documentation required for project close-out and lender draw disbursement.

This level of documentation protects the association, satisfies lender requirements, and provides future boards with complete records of the roofing system installation.
