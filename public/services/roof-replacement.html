When Is Full Roof Replacement Needed?

Most South Florida roofs require replacement every 15–30 years depending on the material, installation quality, and exposure to weather. Common reasons homeowners and property managers choose full roof replacement include:

  • • Age: Roofs nearing the end of their rated lifespan often show wear across the entire system, making replacement more cost-effective than ongoing repairs.
  • • Hurricane Damage: After major storms, widespread damage to shingles, tiles, or underlayment may require a complete tear-off and reinstallation.
  • • Chronic Leaks: When repairs no longer stop water intrusion, the deck, underlayment, or substrate may be compromised beyond spot fixes.
  • • Insurance Requirements: Many carriers now require roofs over a certain age to be replaced before issuing or renewing coverage in Florida.
  • • HOA or Real Estate Requirements: Associations may mandate replacement before resale, or lenders may require updated roofs for financing approval.

If you're unsure whether your roof needs replacement or if targeted repairs will suffice, our team provides honest on-site assessments with photo documentation and cost comparisons.

Our Roof Replacement Process

All Phase Construction USA follows a proven, step-by-step process designed to minimize disruption, ensure code compliance, and deliver insurance-ready documentation:

1. Inspection & Proposal

We conduct a thorough on-site inspection, document existing conditions with photos, and provide a detailed scope of work. Our proposals include line-item pricing for materials, labor, permits, and any structural upgrades required by code.

2. Permitting & Engineering

As a dual-licensed contractor, we handle all permit applications and engineering drawings when required. This includes HVHZ wind-load calculations, uplift specifications, and secondary water resistance details.

3. Tear-Off & Deck Inspection

We remove the old roofing material, inspect the deck for damage or rot, and repair or replace any compromised sheathing. Proper deck preparation is essential for long-term performance.

4. Dry-In & Underlayment

We install high-performance underlayment systems including secondary water barriers and ice-and-water shield in valleys and critical areas. This step protects your home even before the final roofing material goes on.

5. Final Roofing Installation

Whether you've chosen shingle, tile, metal, or flat roofing, we install according to manufacturer specifications and HVHZ fastening schedules to ensure maximum wind resistance and warranty compliance.

6. Inspections & Close-Out

We coordinate all required building department inspections and provide a complete close-out package including permits, inspection reports, product warranties, and wind-mitigation documentation for your insurance carrier.

HVHZ Code Upgrades & Wind Mitigation

Broward and Palm Beach Counties are located in Florida's High Velocity Hurricane Zone, requiring enhanced roofing standards. When we perform roof replacement, we ensure your new roof meets or exceeds all current HVHZ requirements including:

  • • Enhanced Fastening Schedules: Increased nailing patterns and fastener specifications designed for extreme wind uplift.
  • • Secondary Water Resistance: Self-adhering underlayment that provides backup protection even if surface materials fail during a storm.
  • • Roof-to-Wall Connections: Hurricane straps and clips that anchor the roof structure to the building frame, preventing uplift failure.
  • • Roof Deck Attachment: Re-nailing or upgrading deck fasteners to ensure sheathing remains secured to trusses and rafters.
  • • Opening Protection: Coordination with impact windows, doors, and shutters to create a fully protected building envelope.

Because All Phase Construction USA holds both General Contractor and Roofing Contractor licenses, we can legally perform structural wind-mitigation upgrades during roof replacement — work that most roofing-only contractors cannot touch. This saves time, reduces permit complexity, and ensures one contractor is responsible for the entire scope.

Insurance & Documentation

Florida insurance carriers are increasingly strict about roof age, condition, and documentation. All Phase Construction USA provides comprehensive paperwork designed to satisfy insurance requirements and help homeowners qualify for wind-mitigation discounts:

  • • Wind Mitigation Reports: Official OIR-B1-1802 forms documenting roof deck attachment, roof-to-wall connections, roof covering, and secondary water resistance.
  • • Photo Documentation: Before, during, and after photos showing tear-off, deck condition, underlayment installation, and final roofing details.
  • • Permit & Inspection Records: Copies of all building permits and signed-off inspection reports proving code compliance.
  • • Product Warranties: Manufacturer warranty documentation and installation certifications for materials used.
  • • Clean Proposals: Detailed, line-item scopes of work that boards, property managers, and lenders can easily understand and approve.

This documentation not only protects your investment but can qualify you for significant insurance premium reductions — often 20–45% depending on the carrier and upgrades performed.
