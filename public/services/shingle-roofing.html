Architectural vs 3-Tab Shingles

When most homeowners think of "shingle roofing," they're thinking of asphalt-based roofing products. However, there are significant differences in quality, performance, and lifespan between basic 3-tab shingles and modern architectural shingles.

Why We Prefer Architectural Shingles

All Phase Construction USA primarily installs architectural shingles (also called dimensional or laminate shingles) rather than basic 3-tab shingles. Here's why:

  • • Superior Wind Resistance: Architectural shingles are heavier, thicker, and more wind-resistant than 3-tab shingles. When installed to HVHZ standards, they can meet wind ratings of 110–130 mph or higher.
  • • Longer Lifespan: Architectural shingles typically last 25–30 years in South Florida, compared to 15–20 years for 3-tab shingles.
  • • Better Appearance: Architectural shingles have a dimensional, textured look that enhances curb appeal and mimics the appearance of more expensive roofing materials.
  • • Improved Warranty Coverage: Most architectural shingles come with stronger manufacturer warranties, including coverage for wind damage and premature failure.
  • • Better Value: While the initial cost is slightly higher than 3-tab, the improved lifespan and performance make architectural shingles a better long-term investment.

When 3-Tab Shingles Make Sense

There are limited situations where 3-tab shingles may be appropriate — typically on rental properties, budget-constrained projects, or temporary structures where short-term cost savings outweigh long-term performance. However, for primary residences and properties in HVHZ areas, we strongly recommend architectural shingles or better.

HVHZ Nailing & Underlayment Upgrades

In South Florida's High Velocity Hurricane Zone, it's not enough to simply nail shingles to the roof. HVHZ building codes require specific fastening patterns, underlayment systems, and installation methods designed to withstand extreme wind events. Here's what separates a code-compliant HVHZ shingle roof from a basic installation:

Enhanced Fastening Schedules

HVHZ installations require 6 nails per shingle (compared to 4 nails in standard installations) with specific placement to maximize wind resistance. All fasteners must be corrosion-resistant and driven to proper depth — not too shallow (which reduces holding power) and not too deep (which can tear the shingle).

All Phase Construction USA follows manufacturer fastening diagrams and HVHZ standards on every shingle roof we install. This is non-negotiable and critical for warranty coverage and code compliance.

High-Performance Synthetic Underlayment

Old-school felt paper is no longer acceptable for HVHZ installations. Modern synthetic underlayments are stronger, lighter, more water-resistant, and far more UV-stable than traditional felt. They won't tear during installation, won't absorb moisture, and provide superior backup waterproofing if shingles are damaged or displaced during a storm.

We install premium synthetic underlayment on every shingle roof as part of our Good, Better, and Best packages. Our Premium tier includes upgraded underlayment systems with enhanced water and heat resistance.

Secondary Water Resistance (SWR)

Florida Building Code requires secondary water resistance in critical areas including valleys, eaves, and around penetrations. We install self-adhering membranes in these locations to create a waterproof barrier that protects your home even if shingles fail during extreme weather.

This upgrade is not optional — it's required by code and essential for wind-mitigation credits that can reduce your insurance premiums by 20–45%.

Starter Strips & Edge Sealing

Proper starter strip installation along eaves and rakes is critical for wind resistance. We use continuous starter strips with enhanced sealant to ensure the first course of shingles is fully bonded and cannot be lifted by wind-driven uplift forces.

Roof Deck Re-Nailing (When Required)

If your existing roof deck does not meet current attachment standards, we can re-nail or re-screw the sheathing to the roof framing as part of the replacement project. This is a common wind-mitigation upgrade that improves structural performance and qualifies for insurance discounts.

Integration With Gutters, Ventilation, and Accessories

A complete shingle roof system includes more than just the shingles themselves. All Phase Construction USA coordinates the installation of critical accessories and components that ensure long-term performance and proper building function:

Roof Ventilation Systems

Proper attic ventilation is essential in South Florida's hot, humid climate. We install ridge vents, soffit vents, and intake/exhaust systems engineered to meet Florida Building Code ventilation requirements. Adequate ventilation extends shingle lifespan, reduces cooling costs, and prevents moisture buildup that can damage decking and framing.

Gutter Systems

Seamless aluminum gutters and downspouts protect your home's foundation, landscaping, and exterior finishes from water damage. We can coordinate gutter installation as part of your roof replacement project or work with your existing gutter contractor.

Flashing & Penetration Sealing

All roof penetrations including plumbing vents, exhaust fans, skylights, and chimneys require proper flashing and sealing. We use code-compliant flashing materials and methods to ensure these common leak points are properly waterproofed.

Drip Edge & Ice-and-Water Shield

Metal drip edge along eaves and rakes directs water away from fascia boards and into gutters. Ice-and-water shield (despite the name being more relevant to northern climates) provides critical waterproofing in valleys and eaves where water concentrates.

Shingle Roof Installation Process

All Phase Construction USA follows a systematic installation process for every shingle roofing project:

1. Tear-Off & Deck Inspection

We remove existing roofing material down to the deck, inspect for damage, and repair or replace any compromised sheathing. Proper deck preparation is essential for long-term performance.

2. Deck Fastening Upgrades (If Required)

If wind-mitigation upgrades are requested or required, we re-nail or re-screw the deck to meet current HVHZ attachment standards.

3. Synthetic Underlayment Installation

Premium synthetic underlayment is installed across the entire deck with secondary water barriers in valleys, eaves, and critical areas.

4. Drip Edge, Flashing & Starter Strips

Metal drip edge is installed along eaves and rakes. Flashing is installed at all penetrations and wall transitions. Starter strips are applied along eaves with enhanced sealant.

5. Shingle Installation

Shingles are installed from eave to ridge using HVHZ 6-nail fastening patterns. Every course is aligned, properly spaced, and sealed according to manufacturer specifications.

6. Ridge Vents & Caps

Ridge vents are installed for proper attic ventilation, then covered with matching ridge cap shingles mechanically fastened to HVHZ standards.

7. Final Inspection & Documentation

We coordinate building department inspections and provide complete close-out documentation including permits, warranties, and wind-mitigation forms for insurance purposes.
